Neurotransmitter vesicles move along the axon tract via microtubules, they carry within them specific neurotransmitters that are used later on the either inhibit or excite a post-synaptic neuron. The movement of these vesicles is due to a motor protein called, kinesin. We wish to determine the effects of defective kinesin and various neurotransmitter release proteins in C. elegan mutant strains (wild-type, vesicle transport mutation, neurotransmitter release mutation). In the wild-type, I predict no abnormalities in specimen movement or nervous system composition. Unlike the other two strains, where there will most likely be vesicle buildup and neural decay due to lack of stimulation (Reiko Sato-Yoshitake et al.)1. In order to observe these predictions, we shall conduct an ETOH sedation and GFP microanalysis of each strain. In these observations we shall discover the effects of defective motor proteins on the nervous system and the abnormal composition of the cells within said nervous system.
